Volcano

A volcano is an opening in the earth’s crust through which lava, volcanic ash and gases escape. Volcanic eruptions are partly driven by pressure from dissolved gas, much as escaping gases force the cork out of a bottle of champagne.


Causes of volcano

Deep within the earth it is so hot that some rocks slowly melt and become a thick flowing substance called magma. Since it is lighter than the solid rock around it, magma rises and collects in magma chambers. Eventually, some of the magma pushes through vents and fissures the earth’s surface magma that has erupted is called lava.


Diseases occurs during volcano

The most common effects of health caused by volcanic eruptions include traumatic injuries, burns, suffocation, skin diseases, eye injuries, respiratory problems and even death.


Prevention

  • Close all the windows, doors and fireplace.
  • Turn off fans, heating and AC systems.
  • If caught in a rock fall, roll into a ball to protect your head. 
  • Seek care for burns right away. Immediate care can be life saving.
  • If your eyes, nose and throat become irritated from volcanic gases and go away when you are no longer in contact with the gases.
  • Avoid driving, as ash is harmful and the roads may be slippery.

Preparedness

  • Place damp towels at door thresholds.
  • Protect sensitive electronics.
  • Disconnect the tank of rainwater collection systems.
  • Ensure livestock have clean food and water.
  • Wash the engine compartment with a garden house.
  • If there is ash in your water, let it settle and use the clear water.

Recovery

  • Listen to your local radio stations for civil defence advice and follow instructions.
  • Stay indoors and away from volcanic ash fall areas as much as possible.
  • When it is safe to go outside, keep your gutters and roof clear of ash as heavy ash deposits can collapse your roof.
  • Look for and report broken utility lines to appropriate authorities.
