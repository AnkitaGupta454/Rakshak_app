Cyclone

A cyclone is a general term for a weather system in which winds rotate inwardly to an area of low atmosphere pressure. For large weather systems, the circulation pattern is in a counterclockwise direction in the Northern Hemisphere and a clockwise direction the Southern Hemisphere.  


Caused of cyclone

Cyclones are centered on areas of low atmospheric pressure, usually over warm ocean waters near the equator. The warm moist air over the ocean rises from the surface in the upward direction, resulting in the formation of the low pressure zone over the surface.

Cyclones are dangerous because they produce destructive winds, heavy rainfall with flooding and damaging storm surges that can cause inundation of low lying coastal areas.


Diseases that occur during floods

  • The muddy storm waters of cyclone contain multiple risks to human health, Cholera is foremost among these.
  • Vector borne diseases like mosquito borne diseases such as Barmah Forest Virus, Ross river virus, Dengue fever.

Prevention

  • Keep your doors and windows shut.
  • If your house feels unsafe, leave early before the onset of the cyclone.
  • Introduce better flood warning systems.
  • Protect wetlands and introduce plant trees strategically.
  • Improve soil conditions.
  • Introduce water storage areas.

Preparedness

  • Ignore rumours. Stay calm, do not panic.
  • Keep your mobile charged to ensure connectivity.
  • Prepare an emergency kit with essential items.
  • Unite animals to ensure their safety.

Recovery

  • Switch off electrical mains and gas supply.
  • Keep your doors and windows shut.
  • Listen to radio, watch TV, read newspaper for weather updates.
  • Drink boiled or chlorinated water.
  • Reply only on official warning.
