Chemical and biological disasters

Disasters that are caused by the excessive use and misuse of chemicals in industries are called chemicals accidents.

Biological disasters are natural scenarios involving diseases, disability or death on a large scale among humans, animals and plants due to micro-organisms like bacteria or virus.



Causes of both the disasters

A chemical disaster may occur due to both, natural or human-made sources. Chemical disasters are occurrence of emission, fire or explosion involving one or more hazardous chemicals in the course of industrial activity, storage or transportation.

Biological disasters might be caused by epidemic, accidental release of virulent microorganism or BT with the use of biological agents such as anthrax, smallpox, etc.


Diseases occur during the disasters

Chemical- Accidents or incorrect use of chemicals products may cause immediate health effects, such as skin or eye irritation or burns, or poisoning. There can also be longer term health effects from chemicals. When these occur, they are usually the result of exposure to certain chemicals over a long period of time.

Biological- these disasters can lead to diseases like illness, disability, loss of life, starvation, increased poverty, socioeconomic disruptions, and environmental degradation.


Prevention

  • When you notice any chemical leak/accident evacuate calmly and quickly against the wind direction.
  • Keep a wet handkerchief or cloth on face during evacuation.
  • Avoid visiting and become audience to the risky areas.
  • Don’t be obstacle to the people who are managing the disaster.
  • Obey any instruction of the district authorities who will be doing their best to ensure the safety of you, your family and society as a whole and also try to save the property and the environment.

Preparedness

  • Read literature, leaflets, newsletters and television/radio publishing about the properties and characteristics of hazardous chemicals.
  • Participate in all the capacity building programmes organized by the government or industrial units.
  • Prepare a family disaster management plan and explain it to all the family members.
  • Make the family aware of the basic characteristics of various poisonous chemicals and the first aid required to treat them.

Recovery

  • Turn on local radio/TV channels for advice from district emergency operation center/health authorities.
  • Inform others on occurrence of event at public gathering places.
  • Wait for other instructions by authorized people after reaching at safe place
  • Don’t pay attention to the rumors and don’t spread rumors.
  • Keep the sick, elderly, weak, handicapped and other people who are unable to evacuate inside house and close all the doors and windows tightly.
