Landslides

A landslide is defined as the movement of a mass of rock, debris or earth down a slope. Landslides are a type of “mass wasting”, which denotes any down-slope movement of soil and rock under the direct influence of gravity.


Cause of landslide

Slope movement occurs when forces acting down-slopes exceed the strength of the earth materials that compose the slope. Causes include factors that increase the effects of down-slope forces and that contribute to low or reduced strength.

Landslides causes property damage, injury, death and adversely affects a variety of resources. Ex- water supplies, fisheries, sewage disposal systems, forests, dams can be affected for years after a slide event.


Diseases occur during landslides

Muddy –debris flows in alpine areas cause severe damage to structure and infrastructure and often claim human lives.


Prevention

  • Modifying slope geometry
  • Keep drains clean.
  • Keep the weep holes open.
  • Try to avoid construction and staying in vulnerable areas.
  • Do not panic and loose energy by crying.
  • Do not touch or walk over loose material and electrical wiring or pole.
  • Do not drink contaminated water directly through the drains, springs, wells.
  • Do not move an injured person without rendering first aid.

Preparedness

  • Prepare tour to hilly regions according to information given by weather department or news channel.
  • Know how to give signs or communicate during emergency time to flyinf helicopters and rescue team.
  • Stay alert, awake and active.

Recovery

  • Grow more trees that can hold the soil through roots.
  • Listen for unusual sounds such as trees cracking.
  • Locate and go to shelters.
  • Try to stay with your family and companions.
  • Mark path of tracking so that you can’t be lost in middle of forest.
  • Inspect drains for – litter, leaves, plastic bags, rubber, etc.
