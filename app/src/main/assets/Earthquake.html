Earthquake

An earthquake is a phenomenon that occurs without warning and involves shaking of the ground and everything over it. It results from the sudden release of energy and accumulated stress of the moving lithosphere plates.

Cause of earthquake

Earthquakes are usually cased when rock underground suddenly breaks along a fault. This sudden release of energy cause the seismic waves that make the ground shake. when two plates of rock are rubbing against each other, they stick a little. After a while, the rocks break because of all the pressure that’s build up. When the rocks break, earthquake occurs. During the earthquake and afterward, the plates of rock starts moving and they continue to move until they get stuck again.

Diseases that occurs during earthquake

  • Secondary infection of untreated wounds.
  • Increased morbidity and risk of complications related to pregnancy, deliveries and newborns due to interrupted obstetric and neonatal services.
  • Potential risk of communicable diseases, particularly in areas affected by overcrowding.
  • Increased morbidity and risk of complications of chronic diseases due to interruption of treatment.
  • Increased psychosocial needs.

Preparedness 

  • Make sure you have a fire extinguisher, first aid lit,a battery-powered radio, a flashlight, and extra batteries at home.
  • Learn first aid.
  • Learn how to turn off the gas, water and electricity.
  • Make up a plan of where to meet your family after an earthquake.
  • Don’t leave heavy objects on selves,
  • Learn the earthquake plan at your school or workshop.

Prevention

  • Stay calm! If you are indoor, stay inside. If you are outdoor, stay outside.
  • If you’re indoor, stand against a wall near the center of the building or crawl under heavy furniture. Stay away from windows and outside doors.
  •  If you are outside, stay in the open away power lines or anything that might fall.
  • Stay away from buildings.
  • Don’t use matches, candles or any flame.
  • If you’re in the car, stop the car and stay inside until the earthquake stops.
  • Don’t use elevators(they’ll probably get stuck anyway).
  • Turn on the audio and don’t use phones unless it’s an emergency.
  • Be careful of chimneys.

Recovery

  • Check yourself and others for injuries.
  • Provide first aid to anyone who needs it.
  • Stay alert for aftershocks.
  • Emergencies service like medical facilities, ambulance, police, fire department must be alerted to immediate help.
  • Alternate shelter should be kept for the people who had been rescued so that the people may settle immediately during time of disaster. 
  • Rescue others I the emergency they must distribute food, medicines, water, etc.
