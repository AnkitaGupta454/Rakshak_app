Tornado

Tornado is a narrow, violently rotating column of air that extends from a thunderstorm to the ground. Because wind is invisible, it is hard to see a tornado unless it forms a condensation funnel made up of water droplets, dust and debris.


Cause of tornado

Tornado form when warm, humid air collides with cold, dry air. The denser cold air is pushed over the warm air, usually producing thunderstorm. The warm air rises through the colder air, causing an updraft. The draft will begin to rotate if winds vary sharply in speed or direction.


Diseases occur during tornado

The most prevalent health impact that tornados cause are injuries from flying debris, fractures, head injuries, psychological trauma, wound infections, lower hygiene due to home displacement of infectious diseases.


Prevention

  • Don’t sit in the basement where heavy objects like refrigerator rest on the floor above. They could fall through the floor if it’s weakened by the storm.
  • Don’t stand near windows or other glass objects.
  • Drive away from the tornado at a right angle if is far away and if traffic is light.
  • Find shelter in a building. If that’s not possible, lie flat on low ground and protect your head with your arms.
  • Don’t ignore the instructions of facility managers.
  • Go to a stairwell hallway without windows. Cover yourself with blankets.
  • Don’t take shelter under a tree or bridge.
  • When you go outside, watch out for downed power lines.

Preparedness

  • Designate a safe room and put the essentials inside. 
  • Seek shelter immediately.
  • Remove outdoor items.
  • Reinforce your home.
  • Contact your insurance agent.
  • Know the difference between a watch and a warning.

Recovery

  • Check people around you for injuries. Begin first aid or seek help if necessary.
  • Make a detailed list of all damaged or destroyed personal property. Don’t throw out damaged property until you have met with an adjuster.
  • Check utility lines and appliances for damage.
  • Don’t be rushed into signing repair contracts.
  • Keep receipts for additional living expenses such as temporary housing. These coasts are reimbursable under most policies so keep the receipts.
